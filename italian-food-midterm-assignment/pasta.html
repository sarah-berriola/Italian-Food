Pumpkin gnocchi

A delightful autumnal dish that harks back to its origins in Mantova over 400 years ago, thanks to Chef Bartolomeo Stefani. Tender potato gnocchi swimming in a creamy pumpkin sauce with melted provola cheese... and let's not forget that sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top, adding that perfect finishing touch!

Trofie with Pesto

This famous sauce's origins are from a century ago, in Genoa. It was documented by Giovanni Battista Ratto, a friar in Liguria. This flavorful sauce typically features fresh basil, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, pecorino cheese, and garlic. Its vibrant flavor and versatility have made it one of the most famous condiments worldwide, capturing the essence of Italian culinary heritage in every bite.

Bolognese

A classic dish made from tomatoes, carrots, onions, celery, beef, and brotho. Originating from Bologna by the chef Alberto Alvisi around 200-300 years ago, it's a timeless masterpiece has delighted palates for generations. While the tagliatelle pasta creation dates back 600 years from Mastro Zafirano, who drew inspiration from Lucrezia Borgia's beautiful blonde hair.

Linguine allo Scoglio

A seafood delight and a taste of the Mediterranean, originating from Sicily and Southern Italy. This coastal favorite features spaghetti or linguine pasta adorned with a mix of mussels, clams, squid, and large shrimp, cooked with garlic and white wine for a burst of flavor. This dish has usually two variations: one with cherry tomatoes and one without, both finished with a sprinkle of fresh parsley.
