Gelato

The modern Gelato traces its origins in Sicily, around 400 years ago. From its humble beginnings, gelato rose to fame in Naples, which was known to have the best ice cream. In this same city, the gelato on a stick and in a cone first made their debut around a century ago, with many irresistible flavors: hazelnut, chocolate, coffee, and many more!

Sicilian Cannoli

A beloved Italian pastry with roots dating back over a millennium to the sun-kissed island of Sicily. Known to be a pastry for Carnival festivities , it has become a beloved year-round treat. Crafted from a delicate shell with a satisfying crunch, these iconic treats are generously filled with a sweet cream that blends sheep's ricotta cheese, milk, and sugar.

Sfogliatelle ricce

A sweet treat originating from Southern Italy, particularly Salerno and Naples, over 200 years ago from a nun in a convent. These pastries feature a crunchy shell filled with a creamy mixture of ricotta and semolina. Variants include white chocolate cream, Nutella, and hazelnut cream fillings.

Struffoli

A Christmas delight with ancient roots, originated in Naples during the time of ancient Greece. The name itself means "rounded" in Greek. These bite-sized treats consist of fried dough balls coated in honey and sprinkles. It's a festive and flavorful addition to any holiday table, bringing a taste of tradition to every bite!
