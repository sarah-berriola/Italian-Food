Pizza Margherita

Born in Naples in 1889 during a royal visit, it's said that Queen Margherita requested a pizza. Chef Raffaele Esposito created a masterpiece with tomato, mozzarella, and basil, resembling the colors of the Italian flag. This simple yet flavorful combination has become a symbol of Italian culinary tradition and famous all over the world!

Mozzarella

Originating from Campania and prized for its rich flavor, its name comes from "mozzare," meaning "cutting by hand," reflecting its softness. Known as "the queen of Mediterranean cuisine," it's adored for its versatility and enjoyed melted on pizza, in salads, or on its own. Each bite offers a burst of juiciness and warmth, making it a beloved staple of Italian gastronomy.
